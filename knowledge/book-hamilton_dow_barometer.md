# The Stock Market Barometer (Hamilton)

William Peter Hamilton's 1922 classic codifying the Dow Theory, based on the writings of Charles H. Dow.

## Core Philosophy
- The stock market is a forward-looking barometer of business conditions
- Price movements in the averages reflect all known information
- The market discounts everything — news is already in the price

## The Dow Theory (Six Tenets)

### 1. The Averages Discount Everything
- All information (earnings, news, sentiment, insider activity) is already reflected in price
- Only unexpected events matter, and their effect is immediate

### 2. Three Trends
- **Primary Trend**: Lasts 1-3 years, the major direction (bull or bear)
- **Secondary Reaction**: Lasts 3 weeks to 3 months, retraces 33-66% of primary move
- **Minor Trend**: Lasts days to weeks, noise — ignore for directional decisions

### 3. Primary Trends Have Three Phases

   **Bull Market:**
   - Phase 1: Accumulation (smart money buys, public is pessimistic)
   - Phase 2: Advancing trend (improving business, increasing participation)
   - Phase 3: Distribution (public buys heavily, smart money sells)

   **Bear Market:**
   - Phase 1: Distribution (smart money sells, public is still optimistic)
   - Phase 2: Declining trend (deteriorating business, panic)
   - Phase 3: Selling climax (worst news, maximum pessimism)

### 4. Averages Must Confirm
- The Industrial Average and the Transportation Average must move in the same direction
- A breakout in one average is not valid without confirmation from the other
- Divergence between averages is a warning signal

### 5. Volume Confirms the Trend
- Volume expands in the direction of the primary trend
- Heavy volume on up days (bull) or down days (bear) confirms
- Light volume during reactions suggests the trend will resume

### 6. Trends Persist Until Reversal Signals
- A trend is assumed to be in effect until a clear reversal signal occurs
- Reversal signals require both averages to break previous secondary reaction levels
- Do not confuse a secondary reaction with a primary trend reversal

## Hamilton's Key Observations

### On Forecasting
- The market barometer predicts business conditions 6-9 months ahead
- Stock prices peak before business peaks, bottom before business bottoms
- Leading indicator: watch the market, not the news

### On Trading
- Ignore daily fluctuations — focus on primary trend
- Few traders can successfully trade secondary reactions
- The most profitable position is with the primary trend

### On the Public
- The public is most bullish at the top and most bearish at the bottom
- Contrary opinion is a useful supplement to Dow Theory
- Wide public participation signals late-stage bull market

## Practical Application

### Determining the Primary Trend
1. Check the weekly trend of the Industrials
2. Check the weekly trend of the Transportation
3. If both are in confirmed uptrend, the primary trend is up
4. If both are in confirmed downtrend, the primary trend is down
5. If they diverge, remain cautious

### Entry Signals
- Buy: both averages break above a previous secondary reaction high on volume
- Sell: both averages break below a previous secondary reaction low on volume

### Avoiding False Signals
- Require both averages to confirm
- Require volume confirmation on the breakout
- Do not act on the first day of a move — wait for continuation

## Closing Quote
> "The stock market is the great barometer of all business. It does not cause events; it records them. The wise man reads the barometer and adjusts his course accordingly."
