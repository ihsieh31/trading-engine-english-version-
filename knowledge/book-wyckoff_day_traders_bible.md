# Day Trader's Bible / My Secrets to Trading in Stocks (Wyckoff)

Wyckoff's practical guide revealing the strategies of successful floor traders and large operators.

## Core Philosophy
- Wall Street is a game of transfer — wealth transfers from the many to the few
- The "crowd" is almost always wrong at major turning points
- Professional traders exploit predictable amateur mistakes

## The Nine Tests for Buying

Before buying any stock, ask:
1. Is the market trend bullish? (Check averages)
2. Is the industry group strong?
3. Is the individual stock stronger than the market?
4. Is the stock in a technical position to advance? (after accumulation)
5. Is there evidence of large operator buying?
6. Is volume confirming the price action?
7. Are bullish news and sentiment absent or fading?
8. Is the risk/reward ratio favorable? (stop-loss clearly defined)
9. Do the longer-term charts support the trade?

## Reading the Tape

### What to Watch
- **Size of trades**: Large blocks indicate professional activity
- **Speed of execution**: Fast markets show urgency
- **Price spread**: Wide spreads indicate volatility, tight spreads indicate control
- **Time and Sales**: Track every print to detect accumulation patterns

### Operator Tactics to Know
- **Marking up/down**: Operators push price to trigger stops
- **Wash sales**: Creating artificial volume to attract attention
- **Matched orders**: Creating the appearance of activity
- **Painting the tape**: Printing trades at strategic prices
- **Stop running**: deliberately triggering stops to accumulate shares

### Traps to Avoid
- **Bull trap**: Breakout above resistance that reverses immediately
- **Bear trap**: Breakdown below support that reverses immediately
- **Trading range traps**: False moves in either direction that snap back

## The Secrets of Successful Traders

### 1. Trade Like a Business
   - Keep records, analyze results, refine process
   - Treat trading as a profession, not a gamble

### 2. Think Independently
   - Ignore tips, news, and crowd opinion
   - Form your own judgment from the tape
   - If you can't decide, stay out

### 3. Trade Your Conviction
   - Half-hearted trades lose money
   - Enter fully when all tests align
   - Scale in only when uncertain

### 4. Know When to Rest
   - Forcing trades after losses destroys accounts
   - The best traders wait for their pitch
   - Overtrading is the #1 cause of failure

### 5. Study Your Mistakes
   - Every loss is a lesson
   - Review all closed trades weekly
   - Identify patterns in losing trades

## Practical Aphorisms
- "The tape is the final authority" — never argue with price
- "Water seeks its own level" — stocks drift back to fair value
- "A stock that will not go up on good news should be sold"
- "A stock that will not go down on bad news should be bought"
- "The public buys what is currently popular; the professional buys what will become popular"

## Closing Quote
> "There is nothing new in Wall Street. What has happened before will happen again."
