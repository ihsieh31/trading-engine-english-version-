# 45 Years in Wall Street (Gann)

W.D. Gann distilled his 45 years of market observation into concrete rules, time cycles, and percentage-based trading systems.

## Core Philosophy
- Markets move in predictable cycles based on time and price relationships
- Human nature repeats, so market patterns repeat
- Success requires discipline, patience, and strict rule-following

## 28 Trading Rules

### Capital Protection
1. Never risk more than 10% of capital on any one trade
2. Use stop-loss orders on every trade without exception
3. Never average a loss — add to winners, not losers
4. When in doubt, get out of the market

### Trend Following
5. Trade only in the direction of the main trend
6. Never buy or sell on a reaction without a confirmed reversal signal
7. Wait for confirmation — do not anticipate breaks
8. Watch for divergences between price and volume

### Timing & Cycles
9. Study weekly and monthly charts for major trend direction
10. Use anniversary dates — tops and bottoms often repeat on same calendar dates
11. Mark solstices and equinoxes — trend changes often occur near these dates
12. Pay attention to 30, 60, 90, 120, 180, 270, and 360 day cycles

### Entry & Exit
13. Buy at support, sell at resistance
14. Use 50% retracement as the most important support/resistance level
15. Enter only when price confirms — never on anticipation
16. Take partial profits at predetermined levels

### Risk Management
17. Keep capital in reserve — never be fully invested
18. Reduce position size in volatile, uncertain markets
19. Never trade when sick, worried, or emotionally disturbed
20. Keep a trading diary — review every trade

### Gann Angles (Key Levels)
21. The 1x1 line (45°) is the most important — price above is bullish, below is bearish
22. 2x1 (two time to one price): strong trend line
23. 1x2 (one time to two price): weak trend, often breaks
24. Break of 1x1 signals major trend change

### Percentage Rules
25. Key support/resistance levels: 25%, 33%, 50%, 66%, 75% retracements
26. After a 50% retracement, the original trend usually resumes
27. A 100% move from a base often pauses or reverses at that level
28. Triple tops and bottoms are powerful reversal signals

## Practical Application
- Always use multiple timeframes (daily, weekly, monthly)
- Combine Gann angles with volume confirmation
- Use percentage retracements as profit targets and stop-loss levels
- Mark key dates ahead of time on the calendar

## Closing Quote
> "The future is but a repetition of the past. Human nature never changes, therefore the markets will repeat the same patterns in the future."
