# How I Trade and Invest (Wyckoff)

Richard D. Wyckoff's 1922 classic on his 33 years of Wall Street experience, laying out methods for trading and investing based on observing the actions of large operators ("the composite man").

## Core Philosophy
- The market is controlled by large operators (smart money)
- Retail traders lose because they don't understand the game being played against them
- Success comes from studying the tape (price and volume) to detect accumulation and distribution

## Laws That Govern the Markets

### Law of Supply and Demand
- Price rises when demand > supply, falls when supply > demand
- Volume confirms: large volume at key levels reveals operator activity
- Always check: is volume supporting or contradicting the price move?

### Law of Cause and Effect
- A period of accumulation (cause) leads to an uptrend (effect)
- A period of distribution (cause) leads to a downtrend (effect)
- The extent of the cause determines the extent of the effect

### Law of Effort vs Result
- Wide volume without proportional price movement = impending reversal
- "Effort" (volume) failing to produce "result" (price) signals operator trap
- This is the most reliable early warning system in Wyckoff

## The Wyckoff Method

### 1. Determine the Trend
   - Use weekly and monthly charts to establish the dominant trend
   - Trade only in the direction of the larger trend
   - A stock in a confirmed uptrend is a buy candidate, not a short

### 2. Select Strong Stocks (in bull) / Weak Stocks (in bear)
   - Compare individual stock action to the market averages
   - In a bull market: the strongest stocks lead on up days and resist on down days
   - In a bear market: the weakest stocks fall hardest and rally least

### 3. Look for Buying/Selling Signs

   **Accumulation Phases:**
   - PS = Preliminary Support (first sign of buying after downtrend)
   - SC = Selling Climax (panic selling, huge volume)
   - AR = Automatic Rally (bounce after SC)
   - ST = Secondary Test (price returns to SC area, lower volume)
   - LPS = Last Point of Supply (narrow range, low volume)
   - SOS = Sign of Strength (breakout above trading range on volume)
   - LPS = Last Point of Support (pullback to breakout, low volume)

   **Distribution Phases:**
   - Mirror of accumulation: PSY, BC, AR, ST, LPO, SOW

### 4. Timing Entry
   - Buy: when SOS confirmed by volume, on LPS pullback
   - Sell: when SOW confirmed by volume, on LPO bounce
   - Never buy without volume confirmation

### 5. Use Charts
   - Point & Figure charts for precise support/resistance
   - Bar charts for volume analysis
   - Line charts for trend direction

## Trading Rules

### Entry Rules
- Buy in a bull market on reactions with decreasing volume
- Sell in a bear market on rallies with decreasing volume
- Always wait for confirmation — do not anticipate the tape

### Position Management
- Pyramid into winning positions (add on strength)
- Never average a losing trade
- Keep a cash reserve for opportunities

### Risk Control
- Use stop-losses at points where the reason for entering is invalidated
- A stock that breaks below the last LPS in a bull campaign is broken
- If the reason for buying is gone, sell immediately regardless of price

## Practical Application
- Start each day by checking the market averages (trend)
- Screen for stocks that are relatively stronger (bull) or weaker (bear)
- Mark accumulation/distribution zones on charts
- Enter only at LPS in accumulation (buy) or LPO in distribution (sell)
- Keep a position sheet tracking why you entered each trade

## Closing Quote
> "We succeed in proportion to the amount of energy and enterprise we use in going after results."
